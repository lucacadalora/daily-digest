China's Steel Sector Seized by Talk of 'Supply Reform 2.0'

March 2, 2025 • By Daily Digest Research Team • 5 min read

China's billion-ton steel industry is edging toward its biggest shake-up in a decade, with speculation growing that Beijing will order plant closures in response to a construction slowdown at home and a wave of protectionism overseas.

Crude-steel production in China — which accounts for about half of global output — is down about 7% year-on-year in the first two months of 2025, according to China Iron and Steel Association. Beijing's National Development and Reform Commission has said it will soon issue detailed plans in several industries, with statements in February by top officials indicating renewed emphasis on cutting industry capacity.

"We are going to see a Supply Reform 2.0," said Peter Zou, director of steel at Shanghai Metals Market Co. "With the increased pressure from trade barriers, China needs to respond. The industry will be more resilient after going through this current difficult situation."

Citigroup Inc. said in a February report it expected "an unprecedented steel supply reform" in the second quarter, including temporary closures of blast furnaces and capacity cuts of at least 50 million tons. A February meeting between Premier Li Qiang and trade associations mentioned the need for "establishing a long-term mechanism to strictly control total production capacity," according to a statement from China's National Development and Reform Commission.
